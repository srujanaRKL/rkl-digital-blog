Quick Answer

Most US small business SEO packages cost between $500 and $5,000 per month in 2026: roughly $500–$1,500 for local starter plans, $1,500–$3,500 for growth plans, and $3,500+ for comprehensive or competitive-market plans. A real package includes a technical audit, keyword research, on-page work, content, local/Google Business Profile optimization, link building, and monthly reporting. Choose based on your goal, market, and timeline — not the lowest sticker price. Beware anything under about $500/month; it usually signals automated, low-quality work.

SEO pricing confuses a lot of small business owners, and for good reason. One agency quotes $400 a month, another quotes $4,000, and both call it "an SEO package." The difference isn't a scam on either end — it's scope. Once you understand what actually goes into SEO and what each tier delivers, the pricing makes sense and choosing gets easy.

What's actually inside an SEO package

Before comparing prices, know what you're buying. A legitimate monthly SEO package is a bundle of recurring work, not a one-time setup. The core components are:

  • Technical SEO. Site speed, crawlability, indexing, mobile, and structured data — the foundation covered in our small business SEO guide.
  • Keyword research & strategy. Finding the terms your customers actually search and mapping them to pages.
  • On-page optimization. Titles, headings, internal links, and content structure tuned for both Google and AI engines.
  • Content creation. New pages and blog posts that answer real customer questions and earn rankings.
  • Local SEO. Google Business Profile, citations, and reviews for businesses that serve an area.
  • Link building / digital PR. Earning mentions and links that build authority.
  • Reporting. Monthly transparency on rankings, traffic, leads, and what's next.

The insight: Price tracks scope and effort, not magic. A higher tier usually means more content, more link building, and faster progress in a competitive market — not a secret ranking button. If a provider can't tell you exactly what you get each month, that's your answer.

SEO vs. paid ads: where packages fit in your budget

Owners often weigh an SEO package against just running ads. They solve different problems: ads buy visibility that stops the moment you stop paying, while SEO builds an asset that compounds. We compare them head-to-head in SEO vs. PPC, but the short answer is most small businesses need both, weighted toward SEO for long-term cost efficiency.

To decide how much to put toward each, start from your overall budget. A common benchmark is 7–10% of gross revenue on marketing, more during a growth push. Our guide on how much to spend on marketing helps you set that number before you divide it across channels.

Don't forget AI search in your package

In 2026, an SEO package that ignores AI search is leaving money on the table. Customers now ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews for recommendations, and being named there is a distinct discipline — Generative Engine Optimization. Some agencies bundle it, some charge separately, and pricing for AI-specific work is still settling. We cover typical ranges in AI SEO pricing for small business. When comparing packages, ask whether GEO is included or extra — it materially affects value.

How to choose the right package

Use this five-question filter before signing anything:

  1. What's the goal? More local calls, more e-commerce sales, and more B2B leads need different mixes of work.
  2. How competitive is your market? A rural service business needs far less than a national brand fighting for the same terms.
  3. Are deliverables specific? "SEO services" is a red flag. "Four articles, 10 link placements, monthly report" is a real scope.
  4. Is reporting transparent? You should see rankings, traffic, and leads monthly, in plain language.
  5. Is the contract fair? Month-to-month or short terms show confidence. Long lock-ins with vague promises don't.

Frequently asked questions

How much do SEO packages cost for a small business in 2026?

Most US small business SEO packages fall between $500 and $5,000 per month. Local starter packages typically run $500–$1,500, growth packages $1,500–$3,500, and comprehensive packages $3,500+. One-time projects and audits usually range from $1,000 to $7,500 depending on scope.

What is included in an SEO package?

A typical monthly package includes a technical audit and fixes, keyword research, on-page optimization, content, Google Business Profile and local citations, link building or digital PR, and monthly reporting. Higher tiers add more content, AI-search (GEO) optimization, and conversion work.

Are cheap SEO packages worth it?

Packages under about $500 a month often rely on automated, low-quality tactics that deliver little or can even harm your rankings. Affordable is fine; suspiciously cheap usually isn't. Look for transparent deliverables and a clear scope rather than the lowest price.

Should I pay a monthly retainer or per project for SEO?

SEO is ongoing, so a monthly retainer suits most small businesses that want steady, compounding growth. A one-time project or audit makes sense when you need a specific fix — a technical cleanup, a migration, or a strategy roadmap.

How do I choose the right SEO package?

Match it to your goal, market competitiveness, and timeline — not just price. Check that deliverables are specific, reporting is transparent, and the provider explains what success looks like and when.
