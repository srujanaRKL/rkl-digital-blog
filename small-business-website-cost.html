How Much Does a Small Business Website Cost in 2026?

By the RKL Digital Team · Last updated June 17, 2026 · 9 min read

Quick Answer

In 2026, a small business website typically costs $0–$50/month for a DIY builder, $1,000–$5,000 for a freelancer, and $5,000–$25,000+ for a professional agency build. Most small businesses land between $3,000 and $10,000 for a custom, conversion-focused, SEO-ready site, plus $20–$300/month in ongoing hosting, domain, and maintenance. Price varies with page count, custom design, e-commerce features, copywriting, and SEO setup — so budget for the site as a revenue asset, not a one-time expense.

"How much should my website cost?" has no single answer, because a five-page brochure site and a custom e-commerce store live in completely different price worlds. But you can get a realistic budget quickly once you know the three main build paths and what drives the price up or down.

This guide lays out honest 2026 price ranges, the ongoing costs people forget, and how to decide what's worth paying for. If you already have a site, pair this with our guide to the signs your website needs a redesign.

DIY website builders ($0–$50/month)

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and Shopify let you build a site yourself for a monthly fee. They're a fine starting point if budget is the constraint and you have time to learn. The hidden cost is yours — hours spent building, plus weaker SEO and conversion than a professional build. We compare the major platforms in WordPress vs. Webflow vs. Shopify.

Freelancers ($1,000–$5,000)

A good freelancer can deliver a clean, custom site for a few thousand dollars. Quality and reliability vary widely, and support after launch is often limited. Great for straightforward brochure sites; riskier for anything complex.

Agencies ($5,000–$25,000+)

An agency build costs more upfront but bundles strategy, design, copywriting, SEO, and ongoing support — and is engineered to convert visitors into customers. For a business where the website is a primary lead source, that ROI usually justifies the price. It's the path we take with our web design service.

What actually drives the price

  • Number of pages — a 5-page site is far cheaper than a 50-page one.
  • Custom design vs. template — bespoke design costs more but stands out.
  • Features — e-commerce, booking, memberships, and integrations add up fast.
  • Copywriting — professional, conversion-focused copy is often the highest-ROI line item.
  • SEO & AI readiness — building for search and AI search from day one saves a costly redo.

The ongoing costs people forget

The build is only part of it. Plan for a domain ($10–$20/year), hosting ($5–$50/month for small sites), an SSL certificate (often free), and maintenance or updates ($0–$200+/month). E-commerce platforms add transaction fees, and ongoing SEO or content is a separate, worthwhile investment if you want the site to grow traffic.

The big insight: The cheapest website is rarely the least expensive. A bargain build that's slow, hard to find, and doesn't convert quietly costs you leads every month — and often a full rebuild within a year or two. Spend on speed, mobile, SEO, and conversion before flashy extras.

How to decide what to spend

Anchor the budget to the website's job. If it's a digital business card, a DIY or freelancer site is fine. If it's your main way of winning customers, treat it as a revenue asset and invest accordingly — the leads it generates should dwarf the build cost. Either way, prioritize the fundamentals that don't go out of style: fast load times, a clean mobile experience, clear messaging, and a site built to rank. Our overview of web design trends for 2026 covers what's worth following and what's just hype.

A simple budgeting rule

For a professional, lead-generating site, budget $3,000–$10,000 upfront plus $50–$300/month for hosting, maintenance, and ongoing SEO. Spend more if you need e-commerce or heavy custom functionality; spend less if you genuinely just need a clean brochure site to start. The goal isn't the cheapest site — it's the one that pays for itself.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a small business website cost in 2026?

Roughly $0–$50/month for a DIY builder, $1,000–$5,000 for a freelancer, and $5,000–$25,000+ for an agency. Most small businesses spend $3,000–$10,000 for a custom, SEO-ready site, plus $20–$300/month ongoing.

What are the ongoing costs of a website?

A domain ($10–$20/year), hosting ($5–$50/month for small sites), SSL (often free), and maintenance ($0–$200+/month). Premium platforms, e-commerce fees, and ongoing SEO add to that.

Is a cheap website worth it for a small business?

It's fine to start, but it often costs more later in lost leads, weak SEO, and a rebuild. If your site is a primary lead source, a professional, conversion-focused build usually pays for itself.

Why do website prices vary so much?

Price depends on page count, custom vs. template design, e-commerce or booking features, copywriting, SEO setup, integrations, and who builds it.

Budget $3,000–$10,000 upfront plus $50–$300/month for a professional, lead-generating site. Prioritize speed, mobile, and conversion over flashy extras.
