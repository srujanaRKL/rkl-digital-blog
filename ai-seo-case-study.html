What 30 Days of AI SEO Looks Like: An Illustrative Walkthrough

By the RKL Digital Team · Last updated June 17, 2026 · 9 min read

Before day 1: the starting point

Our example business, Northside Dental Studio, is typical of a small practice: a decent-looking website, a claimed but under-optimized Google Business Profile, no schema markup, a thin FAQ, and no idea how AI assistants describe it. When you ask ChatGPT or Perplexity "best family dentist in their area," it isn't mentioned. That's the baseline most small businesses start from.

Week 1: Audit and fix the foundation

The first week is diagnosis, not publishing. The work:

  • AI visibility audit. Test how ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews currently describe the business (usually: not at all, or with outdated info).
  • Technical audit. Check crawlability, speed, mobile, and indexing — the basics from our GEO playbook.
  • Service-page cleanup. Rewrite each service page so the offering is stated clearly and directly, because that's what AI lifts.

Illustrative week-1 target: a documented baseline and a prioritized fix list, plus 3–5 technical issues resolved.

Week 2: Structure and schema

Week two makes the site machine-readable. AI engines rely on structure to understand and trust a business, so this is high-leverage work:

  • Schema markup. Add Organization, LocalBusiness, Service, and FAQ structured data. Our guide to getting cited by AI search explains why this matters so much.
  • Direct-answer rewrites. Restructure key pages to open with a crisp, quotable answer to the exact question a customer would ask.
  • NAP and listings. Make name, address, and phone consistent everywhere.

Illustrative week-2 target: full schema deployed across primary pages and a cleaner crawl profile — foundations that AI engines can now parse.

Week 3: Content that gets cited

Now the publishing begins. AI answers are assembled from clear, question-shaped content, so week three builds the assets that earn mentions:

  • FAQ library. Publish 20–40 real customer questions with direct answers, mirroring how people phrase questions to AI.
  • Cornerstone articles. Draft the first two to three in-depth pieces on the topics the business wants to own.
  • Reviews system. Launch a simple, one-tap review-request flow, since reviews heavily influence which local business AI recommends.

Illustrative week-3 target: a live FAQ, one or two cornerstone articles indexed, and the first review requests going out.

Week 4: Measure, and set up the compounding

The final week closes the loop and sets month two in motion:

  • Re-test AI visibility. Re-run the same prompts from week one to see early movement.
  • Report. Show what was done, what changed, and what's next in plain language.
  • Plan the cluster. Map the next batch of content so authority keeps building.

Illustrative week-4 target: the business begins appearing in AI answers for a few specific, lower-competition questions (for example, a niche service phrased a particular way) even if it isn't yet named for the broad, competitive query.

The honest headline: month one is about building the machine, not harvesting the results. Anyone promising a flood of leads in 30 days is overselling. What good AI SEO delivers in month one is a foundation that starts producing early mentions and then compounds — the real momentum shows in months two through four.

Why start now anyway: because most competitors haven't. How to run your own month one

If you want to start this yourself, follow the same order: audit, fix, structure, publish, measure. You can handle the audit and service-page cleanup in-house; schema, content clusters, and monitoring are where a partner speeds things up. Either way, sequence matters more than speed — skipping the foundation to rush content is the most common mistake we see.
