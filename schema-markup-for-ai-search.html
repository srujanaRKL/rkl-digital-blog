Schema Markup for AI Search: A Beginner's Guide

By the RKL Digital Team · Last updated June 17, 2026 · 9 min read

Quick Answer

Schema markup is a small block of structured code — usually in JSON-LD format — that you add to your web pages to describe your business in a language search engines and AI assistants read perfectly. It tells ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Google exactly what your business does, where you serve, what you charge, and what questions you answer — so they can understand and cite you accurately instead of guessing.

When an AI assistant reads your website, it's trying to figure out what you do and whether to recommend you. Plain text leaves a lot to interpretation. Schema markup removes the guesswork by labeling your information in a structured format machines parse without error — your name, address, services, prices, and FAQs, all spelled out.

It's one of the highest-ROI, lowest-glamour parts of AI SEO for small business. This beginner's guide explains what schema is, the types that matter most, and gives you copy-paste examples you can adapt today.

What schema markup actually is

Schema markup comes from Schema.org, a shared vocabulary backed by Google, Microsoft, and others. You wrap your information in this vocabulary using JSON-LD — a tidy script block that sits in your page's code and doesn't change how the page looks to visitors. Search engines and AI assistants read it behind the scenes.

Think of it as labeling boxes when you move. The contents are the same, but a labeled box ("Kitchen — plates") is instantly understood, while an unlabeled one has to be opened and guessed at. Schema labels your content for machines.

The schema types that matter most

You don't need dozens of schema types. For most small businesses, a handful do the heavy lifting.

OrganizationYour company name, logo, contact, social profilesEvery business (online-first)

Example 1: Organization schema

This is the foundation — it tells AI who you are. Replace the placeholder values with your real details:

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context":"https://schema.org",
  "@type":"Organization",
  "name":"Your Business Name",
  "url":"https://www.yourbusiness.com",
  "logo":"https://www.yourbusiness.com/logo.png",
  "telephone":"+1-555-555-5555",
  "email":"hello@yourbusiness.com",
  "sameAs":[
    "https://www.facebook.com/yourbusiness",
    "https://www.instagram.com/yourbusiness"
  ]
}
</script>

Example 2: LocalBusiness schema

If you serve a physical area, this adds your address, hours, and location so AI can recommend you for "near me" questions — a key part of local SEO:

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context":"https://schema.org",
  "@type":"LocalBusiness",
  "name":"Your Business Name",
  "image":"https://www.yourbusiness.com/storefront.jpg",
  "telephone":"+1-555-555-5555",
  "address":{
    "@type":"PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress":"123 Main St",
    "addressLocality":"Brookfield",
    "addressRegion":"WI",
    "postalCode":"53005",
    "addressCountry":"US"
  },
  "openingHours":"Mo-Fr 09:00-17:00",
  "priceRange":"$$"
}
</script>

The big insight: schema is the most reliable way to make sure AI repeats your facts instead of inventing them. Accurate structured data is cheap insurance against being misdescribed in an AI answer.

Example 3: FAQPage schema

FAQ schema is gold for AI search because answers are often assembled from question-and-answer content. It's central to getting cited by generative engines. Make sure the questions and answers in your code exactly match what's visible on the page:

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context":"https://schema.org",
  "@type":"FAQPage",
  "mainEntity":[
    {
      "@type":"Question",
      "name":"How much does your service cost?",
      "acceptedAnswer":{
        "@type":"Answer",
        "text":"Our plans start at $X per month. Pricing depends on scope; book a free call for a quote."
      }
    },
    {
      "@type":"Question",
      "name":"What areas do you serve?",
      "acceptedAnswer":{
        "@type":"Answer",
        "text":"We serve Brookfield, WI and surrounding communities, plus remote clients across the US."
      }
    }
  ]
}
</script>

How to add and validate schema

  1. Generate it. Write the JSON-LD by hand, use a free schema generator, or let an SEO plugin produce it.
  2. Add it to your page. Paste the <script> block into the page's HTML — head or body both work for JSON-LD.
  3. Use real, current data. Match the visible page. Schema that contradicts the page can be ignored or penalized.
  4. Validate. Run the page through Google's Rich Results Test and the Schema.org validator to catch errors.
  5. Maintain it. Update schema whenever your hours, prices, or services change. Stale structured data misleads AI.

Schema sits within your broader technical SEO foundation — the behind-the-scenes work that makes a site easy for engines and AI to crawl, understand, and trust.

Common mistake: adding FAQ schema for questions that don't appear on the page. Google requires the marked-up Q&A to be visible to users. Keep code and page in sync.

Frequently asked questions

What is schema markup?

Schema markup is structured data — a small block of code, usually in JSON-LD format — that you add to your web pages to describe your business, content, and details in a language search engines and AI assistants read perfectly. It removes guesswork so machines know exactly what your page is about.

Does schema markup help with AI search?

Yes. Schema gives AI engines a clean, machine-readable description of your services, location, prices, and FAQs. That makes it far easier for ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews to understand and accurately cite your business.

What are the most important schema types for a small business?

For most small businesses the top three are Organization (or LocalBusiness for a physical location), FAQPage for question-and-answer content, and Service or Product for what you sell. Article schema helps for blog content, and BreadcrumbList helps for site structure.

How do I add schema markup to my website?

Paste a JSON-LD script block into the head or body of your page. Many website builders and SEO plugins can generate it for you. After adding it, validate with Google's Rich Results Test and the Schema.org validator to confirm it's error-free.

Is schema markup hard to implement?

The basic types are beginner-friendly — you fill in your real details and paste the code. It gets more involved when you connect multiple schema types and keep them accurate across many pages, which is where many owners bring in help.
