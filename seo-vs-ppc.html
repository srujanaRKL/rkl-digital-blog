SEO vs. PPC: Where Should a Small Business Spend First?

By the RKL Digital Team · Last updated June 17, 2026 · 9 min read

Quick Answer

SEO earns free, compounding traffic over months and builds a lasting asset, while PPC buys instant, controllable traffic that stops the moment you stop paying. If you need leads this month or are testing a new offer, start with PPC. If you can wait a few months for lower-cost, sustainable traffic, invest in SEO. The strongest play for most small businesses is a small PPC budget for immediate leads while building SEO in the background — each channel makes the other smarter.

"Should I do SEO or Google Ads?" is one of the most common questions we hear from small business owners with a limited budget. The honest answer is that they solve different problems — and the right choice depends on how fast you need results and how long you plan to play.

This guide breaks down the real trade-offs, shows the math on cost per lead over time, and gives you a clear rule for where to spend first. It pairs well with our broader guide to how much to spend on marketing.

The case for starting with PPC

PPC's superpower is speed and certainty. You can turn it on today, appear at the top of Google for high-intent searches, and have leads by the weekend. It's also the fastest way to learn: within weeks you'll know which keywords and offers convert, which is priceless intelligence for your SEO. Start here if you need cash flow now, you're launching a new offer, or your sales cycle is short.

The case for starting with SEO

SEO is the asset play. It takes months to mature, but once you rank, every click is essentially free — and most searchers trust organic results over ads. Over a year, SEO almost always wins on cost per lead, and it keeps working when your ad budget is paused. Start here if you can afford a few patient months, operate in a niche where you can realistically rank, and want to stop renting your traffic. Our small business SEO guide lays out the full path.

The big insight: SEO and PPC aren't rivals — they're a relay. PPC buys you leads and data today; SEO turns that data into a cheaper, durable channel over the next year. The owners who win run both and let each one inform the other.

The cost-per-lead math over time

Here's the pattern we see again and again. In month one, PPC is far cheaper per lead because SEO hasn't ranked yet. By month six, SEO traffic starts arriving for free, and your blended cost per lead drops. By month twelve, SEO is producing leads at a fraction of PPC's cost — while PPC keeps charging the same rate it did on day one. The lesson: PPC buys time, SEO buys leverage.

How to split a limited budget

  • Tight budget, need leads now: 70% PPC / 30% SEO. Keep the lights on while you plant seeds.
  • Balanced, 6-month horizon: 50% / 50%. Steady leads today, momentum for tomorrow.
  • Patient, growth-focused: 30% PPC / 70% SEO. Lean into the compounding channel.

If your budget is genuinely small, don't spread it too thin. Our guide to digital marketing on a small budget shows how to make every dollar count and where free channels fit in.

Don't forget AI search

There's now a third front: AI assistants like ChatGPT and Perplexity, plus Google AI Overviews, increasingly answer buying questions directly. The same content investments that power SEO also help you get cited by AI — a channel PPC can't buy. That's why we treat SEO spend as doing double duty in 2026.

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between SEO and PPC?

SEO earns free organic rankings over time and builds a lasting asset. PPC buys paid placements that appear instantly but stop when you stop paying. SEO compounds; PPC delivers immediate, controllable traffic.

Should a small business start with SEO or PPC?

If you need leads this month or are testing offers, start with PPC. If you want sustainable, lower-cost traffic and can wait, invest in SEO. Most run a small PPC budget while building SEO in the background.

Is SEO cheaper than PPC?

Over time, yes. SEO has higher upfront effort but near-zero cost per click once you rank. PPC is cheaper and faster to start, but charges for every visitor forever.

How long does SEO take to beat PPC on ROI?

Usually 6–12 months, after which organic traffic produces leads without ongoing ad spend. PPC stays valuable for speed, testing, and high-intent terms.

Can I do both SEO and PPC at the same time?

Yes, and it's often best. PPC delivers leads now and reveals which keywords convert; SEO builds a durable, lower-cost channel. Each makes the other more effective.
