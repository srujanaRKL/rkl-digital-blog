How to Get Your Business Recommended by ChatGPT (2026)

By the RKL Digital Team · Last updated June 17, 2026 · 9 min read

Quick Answer

To get recommended by ChatGPT, make your business the clearest, most trustworthy answer to the questions customers ask: publish direct-answer content, add structured data (schema), keep your business details consistent everywhere, earn reviews and reputable mentions, and stay fresh. ChatGPT names businesses it understands and trusts. There's no ad slot — you earn the recommendation by being genuinely the best-described, most-vouched-for option in your category.

More than 700 million people use ChatGPT every week, and a growing share of them ask it for recommendations — for a contractor, an accountant, a software tool, a local clinic. When ChatGPT names a business in that answer, the user often acts on it without ever opening Google. The question every small business owner should be asking is simple: how do we become the business it names?

The good news is that getting recommended by ChatGPT isn't luck or magic. It follows a learnable pattern. This guide gives you the concrete steps, in order. If you want the broader context first, read what AI SEO is and why it matters.

How ChatGPT actually chooses businesses

ChatGPT pulls from two places: its trained knowledge of the web, and — when browsing is on — live sources it retrieves in real time. In both cases, it favors businesses that are described clearly and consistently across many trustworthy pages. It's not ranking links; it's assembling a confident answer from what it understands about you. Five signals move the needle most.

The 6 steps to get recommended by ChatGPT

1. Write direct-answer content

Structure your key pages so each opens with a crisp, quotable answer to the exact question a customer would ask an AI. "What's the best HVAC company for older homes in Austin?" should map to a page that answers it plainly in the first sentence. AI engines lift clear answers and bury vague marketing copy.

2. Add schema markup

Structured data is how you hand ChatGPT a clean, machine-readable description of your business — services, service area, pricing, hours, and FAQs. It removes guesswork. Our guide to schema markup for AI search shows exactly which types to add and how.

3. Keep your business details consistent

Your name, address, phone, and description should match across your website, Google Business Profile, directories, and social profiles. Conflicting information makes an AI less confident — and a less-confident model recommends someone else. Consistency is a quiet but powerful trust signal.

4. Earn reviews and reputable mentions

Reviews on Google and industry sites, plus mentions in articles, roundups, and directories, tell ChatGPT that real people and credible sources vouch for you. A business that's frequently and positively referenced is far more likely to be named than one with a thin footprint.

5. Build topical authority

One page won't make you the expert. A connected cluster of helpful content on your specialty will. Publishing thorough, internally linked articles signals genuine expertise — the same way this blog cluster works. It's also why getting cited on Perplexity and ranking in ChatGPT tend to rise together.

6. Stay fresh

Show visible "last updated" dates, refresh statistics, and keep your content current. Freshness tells AI engines your information is reliable today — and live-browsing answers will surface recent, well-maintained pages faster.

The big insight: ChatGPT recommends the business it can describe most confidently. Your job isn't to game an algorithm — it's to become the clearest, best-documented, most-vouched-for option in your category. Do that and the recommendation follows.

Frequently asked questions

How does ChatGPT decide which businesses to recommend?

It draws on trained knowledge and, when browsing is active, live web sources it trusts. It favors businesses with clear, well-structured content, strong third-party signals like reviews and listings, and consistent information across the web.

Can I pay to be recommended by ChatGPT?

No. There's no ad slot in ChatGPT's organic answers. You earn recommendations through helpful content, accurate structured data, positive reviews, and mentions on reputable third-party sites.

How long does it take to get recommended by ChatGPT?

Most small businesses see mentions within 4–12 weeks of consistent work. Live-browsing answers reflect new content faster; the model's base knowledge updates more slowly.

Does schema markup help with ChatGPT?

Yes. Schema gives AI a machine-readable description of your business, services, prices, and FAQs, which makes ChatGPT more confident describing and recommending you accurately.

Do online reviews affect ChatGPT recommendations?

Yes. Reviews on Google, Yelp, and industry directories are strong trust signals that confirm you're real and well-regarded, increasing the odds an AI names you over a competitor with thin feedback.
